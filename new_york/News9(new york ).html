Central Park cherry blossoms bloom early due to warm winter

Central Park is coming alive with vibrant pinks as the cherry blossom trees in the park have started blooming early due to the warm winter in the city.

Central Park Conservancy, the nonprofit organization that oversees daily Central Park care and fundraising, has launched its cherry blossom tracker due to the early March blooming of the stunning trees.

It will give New Yorkers and visitors the chance to grab some gorgeous snaps and enjoy a colorful stroll in the iconic park.

The tracker details which cherry blossom trees are in the pre-peak, peak, or post-peak stage of blooming.

You will find cherry blossom trees already in peak bloom in Central Park along the path of Kwanzan cherry trees in the landscape west of the Reservoir.
