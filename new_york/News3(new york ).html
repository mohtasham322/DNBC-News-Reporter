Tánaiste Micheál Martin travels to New York and Boston for St Patrick’s Day engagements


Tánaiste Micheál Martin is to travel to the US today to take part in St Patrick's Day celebrations in New York and Boston. The Irish Minister for Foreign Affairs and Defence, who was the Taoiseach until changing roles before Christmas, is one of 36 Irish representatives going to cities in more than 41 countries for the holiday. St Patrick's Day is a key date in the Irish diplomatic calendar, and the government says cultural promotion is central to plans around the holiday. More than 850 official events are taking place around the world this year. He's also slated to attend several events with members of the Irish community in New York. This year's events with political leaders in the US will be largely dominated by the 25th anniversary of the Good Friday Agreement. The Tánaiste's office said a Good Friday Agreement event will take place in every city visited by a minister, including in New York on Monday evening.

On Tuesday, he's also expected to attend a reception to launch an annual report by the American Chamber of Commerce in Ireland.

His schedule on Wednesday includes some high-level engagements at the United Nations, along with a visit to the New York Irish Centre, a Seniors Lunch, and a reception with members of New York's Irish community. That evening, the Tánaiste will attend the St Patrick’s Day Foundation Annual Gala. On Thursday, Mr Martin will be ringing the opening bell at the NASDAQ with other key players from the city's St Patrick's Day parade, like the Grand Marsal and the president of the parade - an event that will be shown on screen in Times Square. Thursday's plan also includes a lunch at the Ireland US Council and the Irish Funds Conference. On Friday, St Patrick's Day, the Tánaiste will take part in the New York City St Patrick's Day parade, followed by a performance that evening by the Cross Border Orchestra in Carnegie Hall.

On Saturday, Tánaiste Micheál Martin will go to Boston to address a John F Kennedy Library event marking a few milestones - St Patrick’s Day, the anniversary of the Good Friday Agreement and the 60th anniversary of President Kennedy’s visit to Ireland. Next Sunday, Mr Martin is due to meet Congressman Richie Neale in Holyoke, before taking part in the Holyoke St Patrick’s Day parade. Claire Cronin, the US Ambassador to Ireland, is also due to be presented with the Holyoke St Patrick’s Day Ambassador Award at the event. The award is aimed at honoring the work of someone who promotes the relationship between the people of Ireland and the United States.
